CYBER THREATS ON DIGITAL PLATFORMS

PHISHING

Attackers send fake emails, text messages, or websites that appear legitimate to trick users into revealing sensitive information ( password, bank details,or personal information).

Example: An email pretending to be from your bank asking you to verify your account.

MALWARE

Malware is malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to a computer or network. Common types include viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and ransomware.

RANSOMWARE

A type of malware the encrypts a victims\s files and demands payment ( a ransom ) to restore access. It can also affect individuals, business, hospitals, and government organizations.

IDENTITY THEFT

Cybercriminals steal personal information such as national ID, password, or bank details and used them to impersonate the victim for financial or other fraudulent purposes.

PASSWORD ATTACKS

Attackers attempt ot guess or steal password using method such as brute-force attacks, dictionary attacks, or credential stuffing.

SOCIAL ENGINEERING

Criminals manipulate people into revealing confidential information or performing actions that compromise security.

Example: Someone pretending to be IT support and asking for your login credentials.

DATA BREACHES

Unauthorized individuals gain access to confidential data stored by organizations, exposing customer information, financial records, or business secrets

DENIAL-OF-SERVICE (DoS) AND DISTRIBUTED DENIAL-OF-SERVICE (DDoS) ATTACKS

Attackers overwhelm a website or online service with expensive traffics, making it unavailable to legitimate to users.

FAKE WEBSITES AND ONLINE SCAMS

Fraudsters creates fake websites or online stores to steal money or personal information from unsuspecting users.

CYBERBULLING AND ONLINE HARASSMENT

The used og digital platforms to threaten, intimidate, or harass individuals through messages, posts, or social media.
